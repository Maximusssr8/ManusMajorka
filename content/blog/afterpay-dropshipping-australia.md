# Afterpay for Dropshipping: How Australian Sellers Add Afterpay to Their Store

Afterpay is not optional for Australian ecommerce. It is an expectation. Over 3.6 million active Afterpay users exist in Australia, and for online stores targeting Australian consumers, not offering buy-now-pay-later (BNPL) is equivalent to not accepting credit cards a decade ago. You will lose sales.

For dropshippers specifically, Afterpay introduces some unique considerations around merchant fees, cash flow timing, and margin impact. This guide covers everything: how Afterpay works from the merchant side, how to set it up on Shopify and WooCommerce, the real impact on conversion rates, and whether the fees are worth it for your business.

## How Afterpay Works for Merchants

### The Customer Experience

When a customer uses Afterpay at checkout, they pay 25% of the order value upfront and the remaining 75% in three fortnightly instalments. The customer pays no interest or fees (provided they make payments on time).

### The Merchant Experience

As a merchant, here is what happens:

1. **Customer selects Afterpay at checkout** and completes a brief approval process.
2. **Afterpay pays you the full order amount** (minus their merchant fee) — you do not wait for the customer to complete their instalments.
3. **Settlement occurs within 48 hours** of the transaction, though the exact timing depends on your agreement with Afterpay.
4. **Afterpay assumes the credit risk** — if the customer defaults on their instalments, that is Afterpay's problem, not yours.

This is the critical point for dropshippers: you receive the full payment (minus fees) upfront, which means your cash flow is not tied up waiting for customer instalments.

## Afterpay Merchant Fees

Afterpay's merchant fee structure for Australian businesses typically includes:

- **Transaction fee**: 4–6% of the order value (varies based on your business size and negotiation)
- **Fixed fee per transaction**: $0.30 AUD
- **No setup fees or monthly fees** for standard integrations

For a typical dropshipping order of $60 AUD, you would pay approximately:
- Transaction fee (at 5%): $3.00
- Fixed fee: $0.30
- **Total Afterpay cost: $3.30 AUD**

### How This Compares to Other Payment Methods

| Payment Method | Approximate Fee on $60 AUD Order |
|---|---|
| Shopify Payments | $1.35 (1.75% + $0.30) |
| PayPal | $1.86 (2.6% + $0.30) |
| Afterpay | $3.30 (5% + $0.30) |
| Zip Pay | $3.30 (approx. 5% + $0.30) |

Afterpay is the most expensive payment option. The question is whether the conversion rate increase justifies the additional cost.

## Does Afterpay Actually Increase Conversions?

### The Data

Afterpay's own data (which should be taken with appropriate scepticism as it is self-reported) claims:
- **30% increase in average order value** for merchants who add Afterpay
- **22% increase in conversion rates** compared to stores without BNPL options

Independent research from the Reserve Bank of Australia and various retail analyses suggests the real impact is more modest but still significant:
- **10–20% conversion rate uplift** for stores in the $30–$200 AUD price range
- **15–25% increase in average order value** as customers feel more comfortable spending more when payments are split

### Why It Works Particularly Well for Dropshipping

Dropshipping stores often lack the brand trust of established retailers. Afterpay provides a trust signal — customers know that Afterpay has vetted the merchant, and they have the security of Afterpay's buyer protection policies. This trust transfer is especially valuable for new dropshipping stores.

Additionally, the typical dropshipping price point ($30–$120 AUD) sits in Afterpay's sweet spot. At these prices, splitting payments into four instalments of $7.50–$30 makes the purchase feel significantly more accessible.

### When the Fees Are Not Worth It

If your average order value is below $20 AUD, the Afterpay fees eat too heavily into already thin margins. For very low-ticket items, the fixed $0.30 fee plus the percentage makes BNPL uneconomical.

## How to Set Up Afterpay on Shopify

### Step 1: Apply for an Afterpay Merchant Account

1. Visit [Afterpay's merchant sign-up page](https://www.afterpay.com/en-AU/for-merchants).
2. Complete the application form with your business details (ABN, business name, website URL).
3. Afterpay will review your application. Approval typically takes 3–10 business days.
4. You will need a live website with products, shipping information, and clear returns policy to be approved.

**Important for dropshippers:** Afterpay may scrutinise dropshipping businesses more closely. Ensure your store looks professional, has clear policies, and does not use generic supplier images. Having a well-branded store significantly improves your approval chances — Majorka's [Brand Builder](https://manus-majorka.vercel.app/app/brand-builder) can help you create professional branding.

### Step 2: Install Afterpay on Shopify

Once approved:

1. Go to your Shopify admin panel.
2. Navigate to **Settings > Payments**.
3. In the **Alternative payment methods** section, search for Afterpay.
4. Click **Activate** and enter your Afterpay merchant credentials.
5. Afterpay will now appear as a payment option at checkout.

### Step 3: Add Afterpay Messaging to Product Pages

Afterpay provides a JavaScript widget that displays instalment amounts on product pages (e.g., "or 4 interest-free payments of $15.00 with Afterpay"). This messaging is critical — it increases conversion by making the instalment option visible before checkout.

Install the Afterpay messaging widget:

1. Install the "Afterpay / Clearpay" app from the Shopify App Store.
2. Configure the widget to display on product pages and cart pages.
3. Customise the styling to match your store's design.

### Step 4: Test the Integration

Place a test order to ensure:
- Afterpay appears at checkout
- The instalment messaging displays correctly on product pages
- Orders flow through properly to your order management system

## How to Set Up Afterpay on WooCommerce

### Step 1: Apply for a Merchant Account

The application process is the same as above — apply through Afterpay's merchant portal.

### Step 2: Install the Plugin

1. In your WordPress admin, go to **Plugins > Add New**.
2. Search for "Afterpay Gateway for WooCommerce".
3. Install and activate the official Afterpay plugin.
4. Navigate to **WooCommerce > Settings > Payments > Afterpay**.
5. Enter your Merchant ID and Secret Key (provided by Afterpay upon approval).
6. Configure the payment limits (minimum and maximum order values for Afterpay).

### Step 3: Configure Product Page Messaging

The WooCommerce plugin includes options to display instalment messaging on product pages. Enable this in the plugin settings and customise the placement to suit your theme.

## Afterpay vs Other BNPL Options in Australia

### Zip Pay / Zip Money

Zip offers similar functionality to Afterpay with some differences:
- **Zip Pay**: Interest-free for purchases under $1,000 with a revolving credit line
- **Merchant fees**: Similar to Afterpay (approximately 4–6%)
- **User base**: Smaller than Afterpay in Australia but still significant
- **Best for**: Stores with higher average order values

### Klarna

Klarna has been expanding in Australia:
- Offers multiple payment options (pay in 4, pay in 30 days, financing)
- Merchant fees comparable to Afterpay
- Growing user base but still behind Afterpay in market penetration

### Which Should You Offer?

Start with Afterpay — it has the largest user base in Australia and the strongest brand recognition. Once your store is established, add Zip as a secondary option. Offering both covers the vast majority of BNPL users in Australia.

## Managing Cash Flow With Afterpay

### Settlement Timing

Afterpay settles payments to merchants in AUD, typically within 48 hours. This is important for dropshippers because you need to pay suppliers before receiving customer payments in some cases.

**Cash flow tip:** When starting out, ensure you have enough working capital to cover 2–3 days of order fulfilment costs while waiting for Afterpay settlements. As your volume grows, settlements become more frequent and predictable.

### Refunds

When a customer requests a refund on an Afterpay order:
1. Process the refund through your ecommerce platform as normal.
2. Afterpay will reverse the remaining instalments for the customer.
3. The merchant fee you paid is typically not refunded by Afterpay.

This means refunds on Afterpay orders cost you the original merchant fee. Factor this into your [margin calculations](/content/blog/dropshipping-profit-margins-australia), particularly if you sell products with higher return rates.

## Afterpay Best Practices for Dropshippers

### 1. Display Afterpay Prominently

Do not bury Afterpay as just another checkout option. Feature the Afterpay logo and instalment messaging:
- On your home page banner
- On every product page (below the price)
- In your cart
- In Facebook and Instagram ad copy ("Available with Afterpay")

### 2. Set Minimum Order Values

Consider setting a minimum order value for Afterpay eligibility (e.g., $30 AUD minimum). This protects your margins on very low-ticket items where the fees are disproportionate.

### 3. Use Afterpay in Your Marketing

Afterpay has a directory of merchants on their app and website. Being listed there gives you free exposure to millions of Australian Afterpay users who browse the directory looking for stores. Ensure your listing is complete with logo, description, and category tags.

### 4. Monitor Afterpay-Specific Metrics

Track separately:
- What percentage of orders use Afterpay vs other payment methods
- Average order value for Afterpay orders vs non-Afterpay orders
- Conversion rate for sessions where Afterpay messaging is visible

This data tells you whether the higher merchant fees are delivering proportional value.

### 5. Consider Afterpay in Your Pricing Strategy

Some dropshippers build the Afterpay fee into their base pricing across all products, effectively spreading the cost across all customers. Others absorb it as a cost of doing business. The right approach depends on your margins and competition.
