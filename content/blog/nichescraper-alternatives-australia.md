# NicheScraper Alternatives for Australian Sellers: Majorka vs The Rest

If you've been researching product research tools for your dropshipping business, NicheScraper has probably come up more than once. It's one of the more established tools in the space, and for good reason — it does a solid job of identifying trending products from platforms like AliExpress and Shopify.

But here's the issue for Australian sellers: NicheScraper, like most product research tools, is built primarily for the US market. Pricing is in USD, supplier databases lean heavily toward US-friendly fulfilment, and the analytics don't account for Australian-specific factors like GST, AUD pricing, or local shipping costs.

This guide compares the top product research tools available in 2025, with a specific focus on what matters for Australian dropshippers. We'll look at NicheScraper, Sell The Trend, Ecomhunt, Dropship Spy, and Majorka AI — covering features, pricing (converted to AUD where possible), and honest pros and cons.

## What Australian Sellers Actually Need From a Research Tool

Before comparing tools, let's establish what features matter specifically for the Australian market:

1. **AUD pricing data**: Seeing product costs and potential selling prices in Australian dollars, not USD
2. **AU supplier options**: Access to suppliers who ship quickly to Australia, not just US warehouses
3. **GST calculations**: Margin calculations that factor in Australia's 10% GST
4. **Local market trends**: Understanding what's trending in Australia, not just the US
5. **AU shipping cost data**: Realistic shipping estimates for Australian delivery
6. **BNPL compatibility**: Understanding which products work well with Afterpay/Zip price points
7. **Competitive analysis**: Seeing what Australian stores are selling, not just global data

With these criteria in mind, let's evaluate each tool.

## NicheScraper

### Overview
NicheScraper is a well-known product research tool that scrapes data from Shopify stores and AliExpress to identify trending products. It's been around since 2018 and has built a large user base.

### Key Features
- **Product Scraper**: Scans Shopify stores for trending products with sales velocity data
- **Hand-Picked Products**: Curated list of winning products updated regularly
- **Store Analysis**: Analyse any Shopify store's product catalogue and estimated revenue
- **AliExpress Integration**: Direct product links with supplier ratings
- **Chrome Extension**: Quick product analysis while browsing

### Pricing
- Free plan: Limited access
- Pro: $49.95 USD/month (~$78 AUD/month)

### Pros
- Large database of products and stores
- Reliable data scraping from Shopify
- Good Chrome extension for quick research
- Regular updates to the hand-picked product list
- Established tool with a proven track record

### Cons for Australian Sellers
- All pricing displayed in USD — requires manual conversion
- No GST margin calculations
- Supplier database favours US-based fulfilment centres
- Shipping time estimates are US-centric
- No Australian store-specific competitive data
- No AU supplier recommendations
- No local trend data for the Australian market

### Verdict for AU Sellers
NicheScraper is a competent tool for general product research, but you'll need to do significant manual work to adapt its insights to the Australian market. Every margin calculation needs GST adjustment, every shipping estimate needs recalculation, and the competitive landscape data doesn't reflect what Australian consumers actually see.

## Sell The Trend

### Overview
Sell The Trend positions itself as an all-in-one dropshipping platform with product research, store building, and order fulfilment. It uses a proprietary "Nexus" algorithm to predict trending products.

### Key Features
- **NEXUS Product Explorer**: AI-powered trending product identification
- **Store Intelligence**: Analyse competing Shopify stores
- **Video Ad Creator**: Basic video ad generation
- **Facebook Audience Builder**: Suggested targeting for products
- **AliExpress and CJ Dropshipping Integration**: Direct sourcing links
- **Order Fulfilment**: Push orders to suppliers directly

### Pricing
- Essential: $39.97 USD/month (~$63 AUD/month)
- Pro: $99.97 USD/month (~$157 AUD/month)

### Pros
- Comprehensive platform beyond just product research
- Video ad creator saves time for beginners
- NEXUS algorithm surfaces genuinely trending products
- Good integration with CJ Dropshipping
- Regular platform updates with new features

### Cons for Australian Sellers
- USD pricing throughout — no AUD option
- Video ad creator doesn't account for Australian audiences or regulations
- Facebook audience suggestions are US-focused
- Fulfilment integration doesn't prioritise AU shipping routes
- No GST calculations in margin estimates
- Higher price point for the Pro tier

### Verdict for AU Sellers
Sell The Trend offers more features than NicheScraper, particularly with its video ad creator and fulfilment integration. However, the same fundamental problem exists: it's built for the US market. Australian sellers are an afterthought in both data and features.

## Ecomhunt

### Overview
Ecomhunt is one of the original "winning product" curation services. Rather than scraping data, their team manually selects and analyses products daily, providing detailed breakdowns including targeting suggestions and ad copy.

### Key Features
- **Daily Curated Products**: 2-3 hand-picked winning products per day
- **Product Analysis**: Detailed breakdown including profit margins, targeting, and ad copy
- **Saturation Inspector**: Check how many stores are selling a product
- **Lucky (Random Product Generator)**: Discover random potential winners
- **Facebook Ad Examples**: Real ad examples for each product

### Pricing
- Free: 1 product/day with limited data
- Pro: $29.99 USD/month (~$47 AUD/month)

### Pros
- Most affordable option on this list
- Curated approach means higher quality product suggestions
- Detailed breakdowns save research time
- Good for beginners who want guided product selection
- Saturation inspector is genuinely useful

### Cons for Australian Sellers
- Very limited product quantity (2-3/day)
- Margin calculations exclude GST
- All pricing in USD
- Ad copy and targeting suggestions are US-centric
- No Australian supplier data
- Product trends may not translate to the AU market
- Limited compared to tools with scraping capabilities

### Verdict for AU Sellers
Ecomhunt is budget-friendly and the curated approach works well for beginners. However, the limited number of products per day and the complete absence of Australian market data mean you're doing a lot of guesswork about whether a product that's winning in the US will work in Australia.

## Dropship Spy

### Overview
Dropship Spy focuses on social proof and ad intelligence, tracking products that are generating significant engagement on social media platforms.

### Key Features
- **Winning Products Feed**: Products with proven social media traction
- **Ad Spy**: Track Facebook and Instagram ads for dropshipped products
- **Influencer Database**: Find influencers for product promotion
- **Review Importer**: Import AliExpress reviews to your store
- **Supplier Recommendations**: Vetted supplier suggestions

### Pricing
- Lifetime deal: $29 USD one-time (limited features)
- Pro: $39 USD/month (~$61 AUD/month)

### Pros
- Strong ad intelligence features
- Influencer database is a unique offering
- Review importer is a practical tool
- Competitive pricing
- The lifetime deal offers good value for casual users

### Cons for Australian Sellers
- Ad spy data is heavily weighted toward US/UK markets
- Influencer database doesn't include many Australian influencers
- No AU-specific features whatsoever
- Review importer doesn't filter for AU-relevant reviews
- Product trend data doesn't reflect Australian consumer behaviour
- Less actively developed than competitors

### Verdict for AU Sellers
Dropship Spy's ad intelligence features are useful regardless of your market, as you can learn from successful ads globally. However, the tool provides zero Australia-specific value, and the influencer database is largely irrelevant for targeting Australian customers.
