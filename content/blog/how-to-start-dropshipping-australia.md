# How to Start Dropshipping in Australia in 2025 (Complete Guide)

Starting a dropshipping business in Australia is straightforward on paper — register a business, build a store, find suppliers, run ads, fulfil orders. In practice, the Australian market has specific legal, logistical, and consumer behaviour differences that trip up newcomers who follow American-centric guides.

This guide covers every step from business registration to your first sale, with Australian-specific details that actually matter. Whether you are starting as a side hustle or building a full-time business, this is the process that works.

## Step 1: Understand the Australian Dropshipping Landscape

Before you invest time and money, understand what you are getting into.

### What Dropshipping Looks Like in Australia

Dropshipping in Australia typically means selling products online via your own Shopify or WooCommerce store, sourcing from overseas suppliers (usually China via AliExpress or CJ Dropshipping), and having products shipped directly to your Australian customers.

The model works, but Australian consumers have higher expectations than many other markets:

- **Shipping speed matters** — Australians are accustomed to 2–5 business day delivery from local retailers. Waiting 15–30 days for products from China is a friction point you need to manage.
- **Buy-now-pay-later is dominant** — Afterpay, Zip, and Klarna are widely used. Not offering these options costs you conversions. See our dedicated guide on [Afterpay for dropshipping](/content/blog/afterpay-dropshipping-australia).
- **Consumer protection is strong** — The Australian Consumer Law gives buyers significant rights around refunds and product quality.

### Is It Still Profitable?

Yes, but margins are tighter than the gurus suggest. After accounting for product cost, shipping, GST, payment processing, and advertising, realistic net margins for Australian dropshippers sit between 15% and 35% depending on the product category. See our [analysis of real dropshipping margins in Australia](/content/blog/dropshipping-profit-margins-australia) for detailed breakdowns.

## Step 2: Register Your Business

### Get an ABN (Australian Business Number)

You need an ABN to operate a business in Australia. Registration is free and takes about 10 minutes through the [Australian Business Register](https://www.abr.gov.au/).

**What you need:**
- Your Tax File Number (TFN)
- Personal identification details
- A description of your business activity (select "retail trade" or "online retail")

You can operate as a sole trader initially. This is the simplest structure and sufficient for most new dropshippers. If your business grows, you can restructure as a company later.

### Register for GST

You **must** register for GST if your annual turnover reaches $75,000 AUD. Below that threshold, registration is optional but worth considering — it allows you to claim GST credits on business expenses.

Since July 2018, GST applies to all goods sold to Australian consumers regardless of the product's origin or value. This means you need to either:

- Collect and remit 10% GST on your sales, or
- Factor GST into your pricing from the start

Register for GST through the [ATO Business Portal](https://www.ato.gov.au/Business/GST/Registering-for-GST/).

### Register a Business Name

If you want to trade under a name other than your own, register a business name through [ASIC](https://www.asic.gov.au/). This costs $39 AUD for one year or $92 AUD for three years.

## Step 3: Choose Your Selling Platform

### Shopify (Recommended for Most)

Shopify is the dominant platform for Australian dropshippers. Plans start at $57 AUD per month (Basic plan).

**Advantages:**
- Extensive app ecosystem for dropshipping (DSers, AutoDS, Zendrop)
- Built-in Afterpay and Zip integration
- Australian dollar support and local payment processing
- Reliable uptime and fast checkout

For a detailed setup walkthrough, see our [Shopify Australia guide](/content/blog/shopify-australia-guide).

### WooCommerce

WooCommerce is free but requires WordPress hosting ($15–$50 AUD/month for decent hosting). It offers more customisation but requires more technical knowledge.

**Best for:** Sellers with WordPress experience who want full control over their store.

### Other Options

- **BigCommerce** — Solid alternative to Shopify with no transaction fees
- **Amazon Australia** — Not traditional dropshipping, but worth considering for certain products
- **eBay Australia** — Lower barrier to entry; good for testing products

## Step 4: Find Your Niche and Products

This is where most dropshippers spend too little time and pay for it later. Product selection is the single biggest factor in whether your business succeeds.

### What Works in Australia

The Australian market responds well to products that solve specific problems, align with the outdoor lifestyle, or tap into health and wellness trends. 1. **Identify trending categories** — Use Google Trends (filtered to Australia), TikTok trending products, and competitor research.
2. **Validate demand** — Check search volume, social media engagement, and existing competition in the Australian market.
3. **Calculate margins** — Factor in all costs: product, shipping, GST, payment fees, advertising, returns.
4. **Order samples** — Always test product quality before listing.

## Step 5: Find Reliable Suppliers

### AliExpress

Still the most accessible option for beginners. Look for suppliers with:
- 4.5+ star ratings
- High transaction volumes
- ePacket or AliExpress Standard Shipping to Australia
- Responsive communication

Realistic shipping times to Australia are 10–25 business days via standard shipping. See our complete guide on [AliExpress to Australia dropshipping](/content/blog/aliexpress-australia-dropshipping) for detailed supplier selection criteria and shipping timeframes.

### CJ Dropshipping

CJ Dropshipping offers faster processing times and has warehouses in Australia for select products. They handle packaging and can add custom branding.

### Domestic Suppliers

For faster shipping, consider Australian-based wholesalers and suppliers. Platforms like SaleHoo and Wholesale Central list Australian suppliers. Domestic sourcing means 2–5 day delivery via [Australia Post](/content/blog/australia-post-dropshipping-guide), which dramatically improves customer satisfaction.

### Private Agents

Once you scale past $5,000–$10,000 AUD per month in revenue, consider working with a sourcing agent in China. They negotiate better prices, manage quality control, and can arrange faster shipping routes.

## Step 6: Set Up Payment Processing

### Shopify Payments

If you are on Shopify, Shopify Payments is the simplest option. Rates for the Basic plan:
- **Online credit card**: 1.75% + $0.30 AUD
- **No additional transaction fees** when using Shopify Payments

### Stripe

Available as an alternative or additional payment processor. Standard rates are 1.75% + $0.30 AUD per transaction.

### PayPal

Still widely used in Australia. Standard rate is 2.6% + $0.30 AUD per transaction. Higher fees, but some customers prefer the buyer protection.

### Afterpay and Buy-Now-Pay-Later

This is non-negotiable for Australian ecommerce. [Afterpay](/content/blog/afterpay-dropshipping-australia) typically charges merchants 4–6% per transaction, but the conversion rate uplift usually offsets this. Zip and Klarna are also worth adding.

## Step 7: Build Your Store

### Essential Pages

Every Australian dropshipping store needs:
- **Home page** — Clear value proposition and featured products
- **Product pages** — High-quality images, detailed descriptions, reviews
- **About page** — Build trust with your brand story
- **Shipping policy** — Be transparent about delivery times (critical for dropshipping)
- **Returns policy** — Must comply with Australian Consumer Law
- **Privacy policy** — Required under the Australian Privacy Act
- **Terms and conditions** — Cover your legal bases
- **Contact page** — Australian consumers expect accessible support

### Design and Branding

Invest time in a professional-looking store. Use a clean, fast-loading theme. Australian consumers are discerning and will leave a store that looks untrustworthy.

## Step 8: Set Up Shipping

### Domestic Shipping

If using Australian-based suppliers, [Australia Post eParcel](/content/blog/australia-post-dropshipping-guide) is the standard choice. MyPost Business accounts give you access to discounted rates:
- **Parcel Post**: From $8.95 AUD (up to 5 kg)
- **Express Post**: From $12.70 AUD (up to 5 kg)

### International Shipping (Supplier to Customer)

When products ship directly from overseas suppliers to Australian customers:
- **AliExpress Standard Shipping**: 10–25 business days, often free or low cost
- **ePacket**: 10–20 business days (being phased out in favour of newer options)
- **Yanwen / 4PX**: 12–25 business days

Set clear shipping expectations on your store. Transparency about delivery times reduces support tickets and disputes.

## Step 9: Launch and Market Your Store

### Facebook and Instagram Ads

Still the primary acquisition channel for Australian dropshippers. Start with:
- $20–$50 AUD per day testing budget
- Interest-based targeting for the Australian market
- Video ads perform better than static images
- Test 3–5 ad creatives per product

### TikTok Ads

Growing rapidly in Australia. Lower CPMs than Meta platforms and strong for impulse-buy products. TikTok Shop is also expanding into the Australian market.

### Google Shopping

Best for products with existing search demand. Higher intent traffic but requires some technical setup.

A longer-term strategy but highly valuable. Create content around your niche to capture organic search traffic. ## Step 10: Manage Operations and Scale

### Order Fulfilment

Automate as much as possible. Apps like DSers (for AliExpress) or AutoDS can automatically forward orders to suppliers. As you scale, move to a sourcing agent or 3PL (third-party logistics) provider.

### Customer Service

Respond to enquiries within 24 hours. Australian consumers expect prompt communication. Set up email templates for common questions (shipping updates, return requests, product enquiries).

### Track Your Metrics

Monitor these key metrics:
- **Cost per acquisition (CPA)** — What you spend to get each customer
- **Average order value (AOV)** — Increase this with upsells and bundles
- **Return rate** — Keep below 5% with quality products and accurate descriptions
- **Net profit margin** — Your actual take-home after all expenses

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Choosing products based on overseas trends** — Always validate demand specifically in the Australian market.
2. **Ignoring GST obligations** — The ATO takes compliance seriously. Get your GST sorted from day one.
3. **Hiding shipping times** — Transparency builds trust. Customers accept longer shipping if they know upfront.
4. **Skipping product samples** — Never sell something you have not held in your hands.
5. **Underpricing** — Factor in all costs before setting prices. Margins that look good on paper evaporate once you include every expense.
6. **Not offering Afterpay** — In Australia, this is expected. Not offering it is leaving money on the table.
