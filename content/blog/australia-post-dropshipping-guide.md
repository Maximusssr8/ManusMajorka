# Australia Post Dropshipping: Shipping Times, Costs & Best Practices

Shipping is the make-or-break factor for Australian dropshipping businesses. Your product can be perfect, your ads can be converting, and your store can look great — but if your shipping is slow, expensive, or unreliable, customers will not come back. In Australia, Australia Post remains the dominant carrier for ecommerce fulfilment, and understanding how to use their services effectively gives you a genuine competitive advantage.

This guide covers everything Australian dropshippers need to know about Australia Post: domestic shipping options, eParcel business rates, MyPost Business setup, tracking integration, returns handling, and how to combine Australia Post with international supplier shipping for the best customer experience.

## When Australia Post Matters for Dropshippers

Traditional dropshipping — where products ship directly from an overseas supplier to your customer — does not typically involve Australia Post at all. The supplier handles shipping, usually via AliExpress Standard Shipping, Yanwen, or 4PX. For details on those international shipping options, see our guide on [AliExpress to Australia shipping](/content/blog/aliexpress-australia-dropshipping).

However, Australia Post becomes essential in these scenarios:

1. **Domestic suppliers** — You source from Australian-based wholesalers or distributors who ship within Australia.
2. **Third-party logistics (3PL)** — You bulk-order inventory to an Australian warehouse and fulfil orders domestically.
3. **Hybrid model** — You use domestic fulfilment for best-selling products while dropshipping slower-moving items from overseas.
4. **Returns processing** — Customers return products to your Australian address.

As your dropshipping business scales, moving towards domestic fulfilment with Australia Post is one of the best ways to improve customer satisfaction and reduce refund rates.

## Australia Post Shipping Options

### Parcel Post (Standard Domestic)

The standard domestic shipping service for non-urgent deliveries.

- **Delivery timeframe**: 2–8 business days (metro areas typically 2–4 days; regional and rural areas 4–8 days)
- **Starting price**: From $8.95 AUD for a small parcel (up to 500 g)
- **Maximum weight**: 22 kg
- **Tracking**: Included
- **Signature on delivery**: Optional add-on ($3.50 AUD)

**Standard Parcel Post pricing tiers (retail rates):**

| Weight | Metro | Regional |
|---|---|---|
| Up to 500 g | $8.95 | $8.95 |
| Up to 1 kg | $11.60 | $11.60 |
| Up to 3 kg | $14.90 | $14.90 |
| Up to 5 kg | $18.70 | $18.70 |

*Note: Prices are approximate retail rates as of early 2025 and may vary. Business account rates are lower.*

### Express Post (Fast Domestic)

For customers willing to pay for speed, or for sellers offering premium shipping.

- **Delivery timeframe**: Next business day to metro areas; 1–3 business days to regional areas
- **Starting price**: From $12.70 AUD for a small parcel
- **Guarantee**: Australia Post offers a money-back guarantee for metro-to-metro Express Post deliveries
- **Tracking**: Included
- **Signature**: Optional

### eParcel (Business Shipping)

eParcel is Australia Post's contract shipping service for businesses. It is the most cost-effective option for regular shippers and is the service most serious dropshippers and ecommerce operators should be using.

- **Delivery timeframe**: Same as Parcel Post (2–8 business days)
- **Pricing**: Negotiated rates based on volume — typically 15–35% cheaper than retail Parcel Post rates
- **Minimum volume**: Generally requires consistent shipping volume (varies by agreement)
- **Features**: Bulk label printing, manifest lodgement, enhanced tracking, integration with ecommerce platforms

**eParcel Express** is also available for faster delivery at business rates.

### International Shipping

Australia Post also handles outbound international shipping, which is less relevant for most Australian dropshippers but useful if you sell to New Zealand or other markets:

- **Economy Air**: 8–15 business days
- **Standard**: 6–10 business days
- **Express**: 2–4 business days

## Setting Up MyPost Business

MyPost Business is Australia Post's free platform for business shippers. It provides access to discounted rates, label printing, and tracking management.

### Step 1: Create a MyPost Business Account

1. Visit [auspost.com.au/mypost-business](https://auspost.com.au/mypost-business).
2. Click **Sign up** and enter your business details.
3. You will need your ABN and business contact information.
4. Account activation is typically immediate.

### Step 2: Connect to Your Ecommerce Platform

MyPost Business integrates with major ecommerce platforms:

**Shopify:**
1. Install the "Australia Post Shipping & Tracking" app from the Shopify App Store.
2. Connect your MyPost Business account credentials.
3. Configure shipping rates and label printing preferences.
4. Orders will sync automatically for label generation.

**WooCommerce:**
1. Install the "Australia Post Shipping & Tracking" plugin.
2. Enter your MyPost Business API credentials.
3. Configure shipping zones and rate calculations.

### Step 3: Configure Shipping Rates

You have several options for displaying shipping costs to customers:

- **Flat rate shipping**: Set a fixed shipping cost (e.g., $9.95 AUD) across all orders. Simple for customers but may under- or over-charge depending on the item.
- **Calculated rates**: Use Australia Post's API to calculate real-time shipping costs based on weight and destination. More accurate but can surprise customers with high costs for remote areas.
- **Free shipping threshold**: Offer free shipping on orders over a certain amount (e.g., free shipping over $80 AUD). This increases average order value and is a strong conversion driver.

**Recommendation:** For most Australian dropshipping stores, a flat rate of $8.95–$12.95 AUD for standard shipping with free shipping over $80–$100 AUD works well. This keeps things simple, covers your costs on most orders, and incentivises larger purchases.

### Step 4: Print Labels and Lodge

MyPost Business allows you to:
- Print shipping labels from your home or office printer
- Generate manifests for bulk lodgement at your local post office or via pickup
- Schedule pickups from your address (available in some areas)

## Tracking and Customer Communication

### Automatic Tracking Updates

When you fulfil an order with Australia Post, the tracking number is generated at label creation. Ensure your platform is configured to:

1. **Automatically email the tracking number to customers** when an order is dispatched.
2. **Update the order status** in your ecommerce platform.
3. **Provide a tracking link** directly to Australia Post's tracking page.

### Proactive Communication

Do not wait for customers to ask about their orders. Set up automated emails at key stages:

- **Order confirmation**: Immediately after purchase
- **Shipping confirmation**: When the label is created, with tracking number
- **Delivery confirmation**: When Australia Post marks the parcel as delivered

## Returns Handling

Returns are inevitable in ecommerce, and how you handle them affects both customer satisfaction and your bottom line.

### Setting Up a Returns Process

1. **Returns address**: You need an Australian address for returns. This can be your home address, a PO Box, or a 3PL warehouse.
2. **Returns policy**: Clearly state your returns policy on your website. Under the Australian Consumer Law, consumers have the right to a refund, replacement, or repair for faulty goods. For change-of-mind returns, your policy can be at your discretion, but offering them builds trust.
3. **Return shipping**: Decide who pays for return shipping:
   - **Customer pays**: Lower cost to you but may deter returns and reduce customer satisfaction
   - **Free returns**: Higher cost but better customer experience. Consider this for higher-value items.
   - **Prepaid return labels**: Australia Post allows you to create return labels that are only charged when used.

### Australia Post Return Labels

Through MyPost Business, you can generate prepaid return labels:
1. Create a return label in your MyPost Business account.
2. Email the label to the customer as a PDF.
3. The customer prints it, attaches it to the parcel, and drops it at any post office or red street posting box (if it fits).
4. You are only charged when the label is scanned.

This is the most professional approach and minimises friction for the customer.

## Cost Optimisation Strategies

### 1. Negotiate Volume Discounts

If you ship more than 50 parcels per month, contact Australia Post's business team to negotiate eParcel rates. Volume discounts of 20–35% off retail rates are common for regular shippers.

### 2. Optimise Packaging

Australia Post charges based on both weight and cubic dimensions. Use the smallest packaging possible:
- **Satchels**: Australia Post sells flat-rate satchels in various sizes. The 500 g satchel is the most cost-effective for small, lightweight items.
- **Custom boxes**: For oddly shaped items, custom-sized boxes reduce wasted space and may lower your cubic weight charges.

### 3. Use Flat Rate Satchels

Australia Post's prepaid satchels offer predictable pricing:
- **500 g satchel**: $8.95 AUD
- **1 kg satchel**: $13.40 AUD
- **3 kg satchel**: $16.60 AUD
- **5 kg satchel**: $19.55 AUD

*Business account rates are lower.*

These are useful when your products fit standard satchel sizes and you want predictable shipping costs.

### 4. Consolidate Shipments

If a customer orders multiple items, ship them in one parcel rather than separately. This sounds obvious, but automated fulfilment systems sometimes default to per-item shipping.

### 5. Consider Hybrid Fulfilment

For your top-selling products, order inventory in bulk from your supplier and store it locally (at home, in a garage, or with a 3PL). Ship these via Australia Post for 2–5 day delivery. Continue dropshipping slower-moving products from overseas.

## Australia Post vs Other Australian Carriers

### Sendle

- **Best for**: Budget-conscious small businesses
- **Pricing**: Often cheaper than Australia Post for small parcels (from ~$6.95 AUD)
- **Delivery times**: Comparable to Parcel Post
- **Integration**: Good Shopify and WooCommerce plugins
- **Drawback**: Less brand recognition with customers; no express option in all areas

### CouriersPlease

- **Best for**: Larger, heavier items
- **Pricing**: Competitive for parcels over 3 kg
- **Delivery times**: 2–6 business days metro
- **Integration**: API available for ecommerce platforms

### Aramex (formerly Fastway)

- **Best for**: Metro deliveries
- **Pricing**: Competitive for metro-to-metro shipments
- **Delivery times**: 1–3 business days metro
- **Drawback**: Regional delivery can be unreliable

### Which Should You Use?

For most Australian dropshippers, Australia Post is the safest choice:
- Customers trust and recognise Australia Post
- Nationwide coverage including regional and rural areas
- Reliable tracking
- Easy returns handling
- Competitive business rates

Consider Sendle as a secondary option for cost-sensitive shipments, particularly if you are shipping lightweight items in metro areas.

## Shipping Strategy for Different Business Stages

### Just Starting Out (0–50 orders/month)

- Use Australia Post retail rates via MyPost Business
- Flat rate shipping on your store ($9.95 AUD standard)
- Manually print labels and lodge at your local post office
- Most orders will ship directly from overseas suppliers — focus on finding reliable ones with decent tracking. See our [AliExpress to Australia guide](/content/blog/aliexpress-australia-dropshipping)

### Growing (50–200 orders/month)

- Negotiate eParcel business rates with Australia Post
- Set up automated label printing integrated with your ecommerce platform
- Consider holding your top 3–5 products locally for faster domestic shipping
- Implement free shipping over $80 AUD to boost average order value

### Scaling (200+ orders/month)

- Use a 3PL warehouse with Australia Post eParcel integration
- Negotiate maximum volume discounts
- Offer both standard and express shipping options
- Consider multiple carriers for different regions (Australia Post for regional, Sendle for metro)

## Common Shipping Mistakes

1. **Not offering tracking** — Always use a tracked service. Untracked parcels lead to disputes and refund requests.
2. **Ignoring regional pricing** — Shipping to Western Australia, Northern Territory, and rural areas costs more. Account for this in your flat rate or use calculated shipping.
3. **Slow dispatch times** — Aim to dispatch within 1–2 business days of receiving an order. Customers notice delays before the parcel even enters the postal system.
4. **Poor packaging** — Damaged goods are your problem, not Australia Post's (unless you purchased insurance). Use appropriate packaging for fragile items.
5. **Not having a returns process** — Set up returns handling before you need it. Scrambling to process a return creates a poor customer experience.
