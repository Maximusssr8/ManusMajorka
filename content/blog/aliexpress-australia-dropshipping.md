# AliExpress to Australia: Shipping Times, Customs & Best Suppliers 2025

AliExpress remains the most popular sourcing platform for Australian dropshippers in 2025. The product range is enormous, the prices are low, and the platform handles supplier communication and payment processing. But the reality of shipping from China to Australia is more complex than most beginner guides let on — shipping times are longer than advertised, customs processes have changed, and finding genuinely reliable suppliers takes more effort than browsing the first page of search results.

This guide covers the practical realities: what shipping times actually look like, how customs and GST work since the removal of the low-value threshold, how to identify reliable suppliers, and what payment methods work best for Australian buyers and sellers.

## Realistic Shipping Times: AliExpress to Australia

The single biggest source of customer complaints for Australian dropshippers is shipping times. Here is what to actually expect, not what AliExpress estimates say.

### Shipping Method Breakdown

#### AliExpress Standard Shipping

- **Advertised delivery time**: 12–20 business days
- **Realistic delivery time**: 14–28 business days (including weekends, that is 3–5 weeks)
- **Cost**: Often free or $1–$5 AUD for most items
- **Tracking**: Yes, end-to-end tracking available
- **Best for**: Most standard dropshipping products

AliExpress Standard Shipping is a consolidated shipping service that uses a combination of carriers. Packages typically move from the supplier to a consolidation warehouse in China, then via air freight to Australia, where they are handed off to Australia Post or a local courier for last-mile delivery.

#### ePacket

- **Advertised delivery time**: 10–20 business days
- **Realistic delivery time**: 12–22 business days
- **Cost**: Free or very low ($1–$3 AUD)
- **Tracking**: Yes
- **Weight limit**: Up to 2 kg
- **Status**: Being gradually phased out in favour of AliExpress Standard Shipping, but still available from some suppliers

ePacket was historically the go-to shipping method for dropshippers due to its speed and low cost. While still functional, its availability is declining as AliExpress pushes sellers toward their own logistics network.

#### AliExpress Saver Shipping

- **Advertised delivery time**: 20–40 business days
- **Realistic delivery time**: 25–50 business days
- **Cost**: Usually free
- **Tracking**: Limited or no tracking in some cases
- **Best for**: Not recommended for dropshipping — too slow

#### Yanwen Special Line / 4PX

- **Realistic delivery time**: 15–30 business days
- **Cost**: Low ($1–$5 AUD)
- **Tracking**: Partial (tracking may stop updating once the parcel leaves China until it reaches Australia)
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious orders where speed is not critical

#### Cainiao Super Economy

- **Realistic delivery time**: 20–45 business days
- **Cost**: Often free
- **Tracking**: Basic
- **Best for**: Low-value items where customers have low expectations. Not ideal for dropshipping.

### What Affects Delivery Speed

Several factors cause variation in shipping times:

1. **Supplier processing time**: The 1–7 days between order placement and the supplier actually dispatching the product. This is separate from shipping time and is often the biggest hidden delay.
2. **Chinese public holidays**: Chinese New Year (January/February), Golden Week (October), and other holidays can add 1–3 weeks of delay.
3. **Customs clearance**: Usually 1–3 days but can be longer during peak periods.
4. **Australian delivery location**: Metro areas receive parcels faster than regional or rural areas. Perth and Darwin are typically the slowest destinations due to geography.
5. **Peak season**: November through February (Black Friday, Christmas, Chinese New Year) significantly slows down the entire supply chain.

### Setting Customer Expectations

For your store, display realistic shipping estimates:
- **Standard shipping from overseas**: 2–4 weeks
- **Express shipping**: 7–14 business days (if available from the supplier)

Be transparent. Hiding or understating shipping times leads to chargebacks, negative reviews, and Afterpay disputes. Customers are surprisingly tolerant of longer shipping times when they know upfront.

## Customs, Duties, and GST

### The GST Situation

Since 1 July 2018, the Australian Government removed the $1,000 low-value threshold for GST on imported goods. This means:

- **All goods imported into Australia are subject to 10% GST**, regardless of value.
- For goods sold through online marketplaces like AliExpress, the marketplace may collect and remit GST on your behalf (AliExpress is registered for this under the "Netflix tax" provisions).
- For goods you sell through your own store, **you** are responsible for collecting GST from your customers and remitting it to the ATO.

### How This Works in Practice for Dropshippers

When you sell a product for $50 AUD through your Shopify store:
- The $50 includes GST (assuming you are GST registered)
- GST component: $4.55 ($50 / 11)
- You remit $4.55 to the ATO in your Business Activity Statement (BAS)

When the supplier ships the product from China:
- If the product value is under $1,000 AUD, it typically clears customs without the customer paying additional duties at the border (because the GST is collected at the point of sale by the Australian merchant — you)
- If the product value exceeds $1,000 AUD, customs duties and additional GST may apply at the border

### Customs Duties

Customs duties apply to certain product categories regardless of value. Common duty rates:

- **Clothing and textiles**: 5% duty
- **Footwear**: 5% duty
- **Electronics**: Generally duty-free
- **Toys and games**: Generally duty-free
- **Cosmetics**: Generally duty-free

The Australian Border Force website provides a [tariff classification tool](https://www.abf.gov.au/importing-exporting-and-manufacturing/tariff-classification) to check specific duty rates.

For most dropshipping products (electronics, accessories, home goods), customs duties are minimal or zero. The primary tax concern is GST.

### Record Keeping

Maintain records of:
- All supplier invoices (in AUD equivalent)
- Shipping costs
- GST collected and remitted
- Any customs duties paid

## Finding Reliable AliExpress Suppliers

Not all AliExpress suppliers are equal. The difference between a good and bad supplier is the difference between a sustainable business and a refund nightmare.

### What to Look For

#### Store Metrics

- **Store rating**: 4.5 stars or higher (out of 5)
- **Positive feedback rate**: 95% or higher
- **Store age**: At least 2 years (newer stores can be fine, but established stores are lower risk)
- **Transaction volume**: Look for products with 500+ orders as a baseline

#### Communication

- **Response time**: Message the supplier before ordering. A supplier who responds within 24 hours is a good sign.
- **English proficiency**: Good communication prevents order errors.
- **Willingness to work with dropshippers**: Ask directly if they support dropshipping (no invoices in packages, blind shipping).

#### Product Quality Indicators

- **Real customer photos**: Look at reviews with customer-uploaded photos, not the stock images
- **Video reviews**: Increasingly available and provide the best quality assessment
- **Detailed specifications**: Suppliers who provide thorough product specs tend to be more professional
- **Multiple SKU options**: A wide range of colours, sizes, or variants suggests a serious supplier

#### Shipping Performance

- **Shipping methods offered**: Prefer suppliers who offer AliExpress Standard Shipping or ePacket to Australia
- **Processing time**: Look for "ships within 1–3 days" rather than "ships within 7 days"
- **Australia-specific reviews**: Filter reviews by Australian buyers to see real delivery times and experiences

### Red Flags to Avoid

- **New stores with no reviews**: High risk of quality issues or scams
- **Prices significantly below competitors**: Usually indicates inferior materials or sizing
- **Stock photos only, no real product images**: The product may not match expectations
- **Slow or evasive communication**: If they are hard to reach before you order, they will be impossible to reach when there is a problem
- **No shipping options to Australia**: Some suppliers do not ship to Australia at all, or only offer very slow methods

### Building Supplier Relationships

Once you find good suppliers:

1. **Start with sample orders** — Order 2–3 units of each product to your own address. Assess quality, packaging, and actual shipping time.
2. **Communicate regularly** — Build a relationship via AliExpress messaging. Reliable suppliers appreciate consistent customers.
3. **Negotiate on volume** — Once you are ordering 50+ units per month, ask for better pricing or faster processing.
4. **Have backup suppliers** — Never rely on a single supplier. Identify 2–3 alternatives for each product.

## Payment Methods on AliExpress

### For Your Supplier Purchases

AliExpress accepts:
- **Credit/debit cards**: Visa, Mastercard (most common for Australian buyers)
- **PayPal**: Available but not for all transactions
- **AliPay**: AliExpress's own payment system
- **Bank transfer**: For larger orders

**Tip for Australian dropshippers:** Use a credit card with no international transaction fees (several Australian banks offer these). AliExpress charges in USD, and international transaction fees of 2–3% add up quickly.

Cards from banks like ING, Bankwest (certain cards), and some Macquarie offerings waive international fees. Alternatively, consider a Wise (formerly TransferWise) debit card for better exchange rates.

### Currency Considerations

AliExpress prices are in USD by default. When calculating margins:
1. Convert product cost from USD to AUD at the current exchange rate
2. Add a 5% buffer for exchange rate fluctuations
3. Factor in any credit card international transaction fees

## Alternatives to AliExpress for Australian Dropshippers

### CJ Dropshipping

- **Advantages**: Faster processing, Australian warehouse for select products, quality inspection, custom packaging
- **Disadvantages**: Smaller product range than AliExpress, requires account setup
- **Best for**: Scaling dropshippers who need faster fulfilment

### DHgate

- **Advantages**: Often cheaper than AliExpress for bulk orders
- **Disadvantages**: Less refined platform, fewer buyer protections
- **Best for**: Bulk purchasing, not ideal for single-item dropshipping

### Banggood

- **Advantages**: Australian warehouse for some products, decent product range
- **Disadvantages**: Smaller selection than AliExpress
- **Best for**: Electronics and gadget niches

### 1688.com

- **Advantages**: Factory-direct prices (significantly cheaper than AliExpress)
- **Disadvantages**: Entirely in Chinese, no built-in buyer protection, requires a sourcing agent
- **Best for**: Advanced dropshippers ready to work with agents

### Private Sourcing Agents

Once you are doing consistent volume ($5,000+ AUD/month), consider working with a private sourcing agent in China. They can:
- Source products directly from factories at 30–50% less than AliExpress prices
- Handle quality control and packaging
- Arrange faster shipping via dedicated freight lines
- Consolidate orders to reduce shipping costs

## Dealing With AliExpress Disputes

Despite best efforts, issues arise. Here is how to handle them:

### Late or Missing Deliveries

1. Check tracking thoroughly — sometimes tracking updates lag behind actual delivery.
2. Contact the supplier first and ask for an update.
3. If the delivery window has expired with no delivery, open an AliExpress dispute for a full refund.
4. AliExpress buyer protection covers you for non-delivery.

### Quality Issues

1. Document the issue with photos and videos.
2. Contact the supplier and request a partial or full refund.
3. If the supplier does not resolve it, open an AliExpress dispute with your evidence.
4. Meanwhile, resolve the issue with your customer directly — refund or reship from a backup supplier.

### Protecting Your Business

- **Build refund costs into your margins**: Assume a 3–5% refund rate when calculating product viability.
- **Maintain backup suppliers**: When one supplier fails, you need an alternative immediately.
- **Track supplier performance**: Keep a spreadsheet of supplier reliability — processing time, shipping time, quality issues, response time.

## Scaling Beyond AliExpress

As your Australian dropshipping business grows, AliExpress becomes a bottleneck. The path forward typically looks like this:

1. **Month 1–3**: AliExpress dropshipping to validate products and find winners
2. **Month 3–6**: Move winning products to CJ Dropshipping for faster processing
3. **Month 6–12**: Work with a sourcing agent for better pricing; hold top sellers locally
4. **Month 12+**: Full domestic fulfilment via 3PL with [Australia Post eParcel](/content/blog/australia-post-dropshipping-guide) for your best sellers

This progression reduces shipping times, improves margins, and gives you a competitive moat that pure AliExpress dropshippers cannot match.
